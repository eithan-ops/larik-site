the premise

For ten years the answer to who's around was a feed, a profile, a swipe.

It wore people out. The problem was never the search. It was being looked at.

Larik is the alternative. An ambient layer between your phone and the room — you say what you're looking for, it listens, without watching.

what it isn't
No profiles.
No photo, no age, no bio. Identity is a quiet mark — yours alone.
No swiping.
Never a feed. One thoughtful suggestion at a time, or none — silence is also an answer.
No exposure.
Five quiet stages of disclosure. You decide what opens, when, and to whom.
No noise.
No badges, counters, or red dots. The app speaks at conversation volume.

Yours alone.

On your iPhone, not in the cloud.

The intelligence runs on your device. Your words never become anyone's training data — not even ours.

Anonymous by default.

We don't know your name, your face, or who you've spoken to — until you choose to tell someone.

Built around the exit.

Delete is at the top of settings, not the bottom. Everything you said, gone, in one tap — no email loop, no recovery email.
